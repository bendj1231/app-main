## Assumptions & Risks

### Key Assumptions
- Pilots willing to pay $100/year for career platform
- Flight schools willing to refer pilots for $20 commission
- Airlines willing to pay $10K-$20K/year for access to qualified pilots
- Network effects will drive growth
- AI coaching provides sufficient value for retention

### Risks
- **Market adoption slower than expected:** Lower pilot acquisition
- **Competition enters market:** Pricing pressure, customer churn
- **Airlines don't adopt recognition score:** Enterprise revenue lower
- **Economic downturn:** Pilots reduce discretionary spending
- **Regulatory changes:** Impact on aviation industry

### Mitigations
- Start with pilot-friendly pricing ($50/6 months option)
- Build network effects quickly
- Focus on neutral positioning to avoid competition
- Diversify revenue streams
- Maintain lean operations

## Conclusion

The financial projections demonstrate strong unit economics, attractive valuation potential, and a clear path to profitability. The base case scenario ($36M valuation) is achievable with focused execution on pilot acquisition, flight school partnerships, and airline adoption.

The multi-sided marketplace model creates network effects that drive sustainable growth, while the diversified revenue streams reduce risk. The high LTV:CAC ratio (9.2x) and short payback period (3.6 months) indicate efficient capital deployment.
