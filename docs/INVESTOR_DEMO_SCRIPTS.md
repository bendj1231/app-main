# Investor Presentation Demo Scripts

**Created:** April 25, 2026  
**Purpose:** Live demo scripts for investor presentations  
**Duration:** 5-10 minutes total  
**Audience:** Angel investors, VCs, strategic partners

## Script 1: Product Demo (3-4 minutes)

### Opening (30 seconds)

**Speaker:** "Let me show you the platform in action. I'll walk through the pilot experience, which is our core value proposition."

### Pilot Profile Setup (45 seconds)

**Speaker:** "Every pilot starts by creating a profile. They enter their flight hours, aircraft types, certifications, and career goals. This data powers our Pilot Recognition Score algorithm."

**Action:** 
- Navigate to profile creation page
- Show profile fields (hours, aircraft types, certifications)
- Demonstrate profile completion progress
- Show real-time score calculation as profile is filled

**Speaker:** "As you can see, the score updates in real-time based on the information provided. This gamification element keeps pilots engaged and motivated to complete their profiles."

### Pilot Recognition Score (45 seconds)

**Speaker:** "The Pilot Recognition Score is our industry-validated credential. It's not just about hours - it considers certifications, aircraft types, career progression, and engagement."

**Action:**
- Show score breakdown page
- Highlight score components (hours, certifications, aircraft, engagement)
- Show score optimization tips
- Display leaderboard/rankings

**Speaker:** "Pilots can see exactly how to improve their score, which keeps them coming back. The leaderboard creates healthy competition and social proof."

### AI Career Coaching (45 seconds)

**Speaker:** "Our AI career coaching provides personalized pathway recommendations. It analyzes the pilot's profile and suggests the best career moves."

**Action:**
- Navigate to AI coaching section
- Show personalized pathway recommendations
- Display job shortage notifications
- Show score maintenance advice

**Speaker:** "This isn't generic career advice - it's personalized based on their specific profile, goals, and market conditions. Pilots pay $100/year for this level of insight."

### Job Matching (45 seconds)

**Speaker:** "We connect pilots with actual opportunities. Our data matching algorithm aligns pilot profiles with airline expectations and job requirements."

**Action:**
- Show job listings page
- Filter by aircraft type, region, airline
- Show job expectations alignment
- Display match percentage

**Speaker:** "Pilots can see which jobs they're qualified for and which pathways will get them there. This transparency is missing in the current market."

### Premium Upgrade (30 seconds)

**Speaker:** "The free tier captures leads, but the premium tier unlocks the full value. Let me show the upgrade flow."

**Action:**
- Click upgrade button
- Show premium features comparison
- Display pricing ($100/year or $50/6 months)
- Show Stripe checkout integration

**Speaker:** "The upgrade process is seamless. Once subscribed, pilots unlock the recognition score, AI coaching, and priority visibility to airlines."

### Closing (15 seconds)

**Speaker:** "That's the core pilot experience. Now let me show you the enterprise side, which is where the network effects really kick in."

## Script 4: Q&A Preparation (Backup)

### Common Investor Questions

**Q: "How do you acquire pilots?"**
**A:** "We use a multi-channel approach: (1) Partnerships with flight schools who earn $20 per referral, (2) Physical presence at airshows and career fairs, (3) Content marketing and SEO, (4) Social media in pilot communities. The referral program is particularly powerful because it aligns incentives."

**Q: "What's your competitive moat?"**
**A:** "Three things: (1) Network effects - more pilots attract more airlines, which attracts more pilots, (2) The Pilot Recognition Score is a proprietary algorithm that becomes more valuable with more data, (3) Our neutral positioning as 'Switzerland of aviation' builds trust with all stakeholders."

**Q: "Why would airlines adopt your recognition score?"**
**A:** "It saves them time and money. Currently, airlines spend significant resources screening candidates. Our score provides a pre-validated signal of quality. We're starting with 2-3 major airlines to prove the value, then expanding."

**Q: "What if pilots won't pay $100/year?"**
**A:** "We've tested this with pilots. Considering they spend $50K-$200K on training, $100/year is 0.05% of that investment. They see it as protecting their career investment. Our free tier also captures leads and demonstrates value before asking for payment."

**Q: "How do you prevent churn?"**
**A:** "Multiple retention strategies: (1) The recognition score creates ongoing engagement - pilots want to maintain and improve it, (2) AI coaching provides continuous value, (3) Social features like leaderboards and mentorship create community, (4) Career relevance spans decades, not months."

**Q: "What's your burn rate and runway?"**
**A:** "Our current burn is approximately $40K/month. With the $500K-$1M seed we're seeking, we have 12-24 months of runway to reach profitability. Our base case shows profitability by Year 4 with 25K pilots."

**Q: "Why now? Why hasn't this been built before?"**
**A:** "Timing is perfect: (1) Post-pilot shortage recovery means airlines are actively hiring, (2) AI is mature enough to provide personalized career guidance, (3) Pilots are more digitally native post-COVID, (4) No dominant player exists in this space. We're first movers with a defensible position."

## Demo Preparation Checklist

### Before the Demo

- [ ] Ensure all features are working correctly
- [ ] Have test data populated (pilot profiles, jobs, applications)
- [ ] Test all user flows (registration, upgrade, job application)
- [ ] Verify analytics dashboard has realistic data
- [ ] Prepare backup accounts (pilot, airline, flight school)
- [ ] Test demo environment stability
- [ ] Have offline screenshots ready in case of technical issues

### During the Demo

- [ ] Keep demo under 10 minutes total
- [ ] Focus on value, not features
- [ ] Highlight network effects and competitive advantages
- [ ] Show real data, not mockups
- [ ] Be prepared to skip sections if running short
- [ ] Have answers ready for common questions
- [ ] Keep energy high and enthusiasm visible

### After the Demo

- [ ] Follow up with any requested materials
- [ ] Send demo recording if requested
- [ ] Provide access to sandbox environment
- [ ] Schedule follow-up meeting
- [ ] Address any outstanding questions

## Troubleshooting

### Common Demo Issues

**Issue: Slow loading times**
**Solution:** Use cached data, preload pages, have screenshots ready

**Issue: Feature not working**
**Solution:** Skip to next feature, have backup flow, explain it's a demo environment

**Issue: Data looks unrealistic**
**Solution:** Populate with realistic test data beforehand

**Issue: Authentication fails**
**Solution:** Have backup accounts ready, use incognito mode

**Issue: Analytics show zeros**
**Solution:** Populate with test data, use mockup view

## Demo Tips

### Best Practices

1. **Start with the problem:** Remind investors why this matters before showing the solution
2. **Show, don't tell:** Let the product speak for itself
3. **Focus on outcomes:** Highlight results, not features
4. **Keep it simple:** Avoid technical jargon unless speaking to technical investors
5. **Be honest:** If something doesn't work, acknowledge it and move on
6. **Tell a story:** Walk through a pilot's journey end-to-end
7. **Highlight differentiation:** Emphasize what makes you unique
8. **Show traction:** Use real metrics to validate the business model

### What to Avoid

1. Don't get bogged down in technical details (unless asked)
2. Don't show incomplete features
3. Don't apologize for the product
4. Don't overpromise on future features
5. Don't spend too much time on any single feature
6. Don't use complex terminology unnecessarily
7. Don't skip the financial metrics
8. Don't forget the competitive advantages
