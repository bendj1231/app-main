## Business Model

### Q: What is your business model?

**A:** We operate a multi-sided marketplace with three primary revenue streams:
1. **Pilot Subscriptions** ($100/year): Premium features including Pilot Recognition Score, AI career coaching, job intelligence, and priority visibility
2. **Referral Commissions** ($20 per pilot): Flight schools earn $20 for each pilot they refer who subscribes
3. **Enterprise Fees** ($10K-$25K/year): Airlines, manufacturers, and training centers pay for access to qualified pilot pipelines and market intelligence

This diversified model reduces risk and enables network effects across all stakeholders.

### Q: Why $100/year for pilots?

**A:** The pricing is strategic:
- **Signals premium quality** - Higher price filters for serious pilots
- **Creates perceived value** - Pilots invest $50K-$200K on training, so $100/year is only 0.05% of that investment
- **Free tier anchors price** - Free profile creation demonstrates value before payment
- **Flexible payment** - $50/6 months option lowers barrier to entry

Our pitch to pilots: "You spent millions on training, $100/year to protect that investment"

### Q: What's your unit economics?

**A:** Our unit economics are strong:
- **LTV (Lifetime Value):** $380 (including referral value)
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** $30 weighted average
- **LTV:CAC Ratio:** 12.7x (well above healthy 4-6x range)
- **Payback Period:** 3.8 months

Flight school referrals have zero CAC, which significantly improves our weighted average.

### Q: How do you make money from referrals if you pay $20 per pilot?

**A:** The referral program is a growth strategy, not a profit center. The $20 payout is a customer acquisition cost that's lower than our CAC from other channels ($30-$100). The strategic value is:
- Organic pilot acquisition through partnerships
- Network effects (more schools = more pilots)
- Brand building in aviation community
- Long-term revenue from subscriptions

### Q: What's your churn rate and how do you prevent churn?

**A:** Our current churn rate is 2.1%/month, well below the SaaS industry average of 5-7%. We prevent churn through:
- **Recognition Score Engagement:** Pilots want to maintain and improve their score
- **AI Coaching:** Continuous personalized value
- **Social Features:** Leaderboards, badges, mentorship create community
- **Career Relevance:** Platform value spans decades, not months
- **Job Matching:** Ongoing access to opportunities

## Competition

### Q: Who are your competitors?

**A:** The market is fragmented with no direct competitor offering our comprehensive model:

**Direct Competitors:**
- **AviationJobSearch:** Job board only, no career guidance or validation
- **JSfirm:** Job board + basic career content, no scoring or AI
- **Pilot Career Center:** Training directory + career content, no job matching

**Indirect Competitors:**
- **LinkedIn:** General professional network, not aviation-specific
- **Airline Career Sites:** Fragmented, no cross-airline visibility
- **Career Coaches:** Expensive 1-on-1, not scalable

**Our Differentiation:** Comprehensive platform (jobs + guidance + validation), industry-validated recognition score, AI coaching, neutral positioning, multi-sided marketplace

### Q: What's your competitive moat?

**A:** Multiple defensible advantages:
1. **Network Effects:** More pilots → More airlines → More pilots (exponential growth)
2. **Neutral Positioning:** "Switzerland of aviation" - trusted by all stakeholders
3. **Proprietary Technology:** Pilot Recognition Score algorithm, multi-AI consensus system
4. **Industry Relationships:** Trust with airlines, flight schools, manufacturers
5. **Data Moat:** Recognition score requires historical data, AI coaching needs industry-specific training
6. **Physical Presence:** Events and airshows create real-world engagement

### Q: Why would airlines adopt your recognition score?

**A:** Value proposition for airlines:
- **Pre-qualified candidates:** Score provides quality signal before interviewing
- **Reduced hiring costs:** Less time screening candidates
- **Better fit candidates:** Score based on relevant criteria
- **Market intelligence:** Visibility into pilot supply and trends

We're starting with 2-3 major airlines in pilot programs to prove value, then expanding. Once airlines adopt the score, it creates a powerful network effect.

### Q: What if a large tech company enters this market?

**A:** We have several advantages:
- **Domain expertise:** Aviation-specific knowledge and relationships
- **Industry trust:** Neutral positioning with airlines and schools
- **Proprietary data:** Recognition score algorithm built on aviation data
- **Network effects:** Critical mass of pilots and airlines
- **First-mover advantage:** Established brand and partnerships

Large tech companies struggle with domain-specific markets due to lack of industry relationships and trust.

## Product & Technology

### Q: What is the Pilot Recognition Score?

**A:** The Pilot Recognition Score is our industry-validated credential that quantifies a pilot's career readiness and quality. It considers:
- Flight hours and experience
- Certifications and ratings
- Aircraft types
- Career progression
- Engagement with platform
- Skill assessments (future)

The score is displayed as a number (0-1000) with tier badges (Iron, Steel, Copper, Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum). Pilots compete on leaderboards and share scores socially, driving engagement and viral growth.

### Q: How does your AI coaching work?

**A:** Our AI career coaching uses a multi-AI consensus system:
- Multiple AI models analyze pilot profiles
- Consensus-based recommendations for career pathways
- Personalized advice based on goals, skills, and market conditions
- Real-time market intelligence on job shortages and opportunities
- Score optimization guidance

The AI is trained on aviation industry data and improves with more platform data, creating a data moat.

### Q: What's your technology stack?

**A:** Modern, scalable stack:
- **Frontend:** React/TypeScript, TailwindCSS
- **Backend:** Supabase (PostgreSQL, Auth, Storage)
- **Edge Functions:** Deno-based
- **Deployment:** Vercel
- **AI/ML:** Multiple AI APIs with consensus system
- **Payment:** Stripe for subscriptions

This stack provides scalability, security, and cost efficiency.

### Q: How do you ensure data security?

**A:** Security is critical with pilot career data:
- Row-level security (RLS) on all database tables
- End-to-end encryption for sensitive data
- Compliance with aviation industry standards
- Regular security audits
- Rate limiting and input validation
- Device fingerprinting for fraud prevention

## Risks

### Q: What are your biggest risks?

**A:** Key risks and mitigations:

**Risk 1: Airlines don't adopt recognition score**
- **Mitigation:** Start with 2-3 major airlines in pilot programs, prove value, expand
- **Backup:** Position score as pilot credential even without airline adoption

**Risk 2: AI advice not valuable enough**
- **Mitigation:** Use real industry data, personalize heavily, iterate based on feedback
- **Backup:** Emphasize other value (recognition, connections, events)

**Risk 3: Can't get enough pilots**
- **Mitigation:** Aggressive event strategy, referral program, partnerships
- **Backup:** Start with specific regions/aircraft types, expand gradually

**Risk 4: Competition**
- **Mitigation:** Build network effects, focus on neutral positioning, move fast
- **Backup:** Differentiate through assessment and social reputation features

**A:** We've tested this with pilots. Considering they spend $50K-$200K on training, $100/year is 0.05% of that investment. They see it as protecting their career investment. Our free tier also captures leads and demonstrates value before asking for payment.
