# Shark Tank Pitch - Kevin O'Leary Conversation

## Core Problem: The Clogged Pipeline (Four-Floor Tower)

### Floor 0: Graduates
- 200 hours, promised airline jobs that never materialize
- Line to instructor positions backed up 2-3 years
- Batch of 2015 still waiting
- $50,000 investment sits unused

### Floor 1: Flight Instructors
- 5,000-6,000 hours, 15 years experience
- Stuck because nobody's leaving Floor 2
- Want recognition for what they've built
- Some try to jump ahead, realize requirements differ, get sent back
- Wasted time

### Floor 2: The Recognition Gap
- This is the collapse point
- Everyone fighting for recognition, pathways, expectations
- Industry lacks communication
- Pilots don't know what's required
- They fly blind
- This is where PilotRecognition provides the framework

### Floor 3: Airline Pilots
- 12+ years, bored, want change but trapped by seniority sacrifice
- Captain goes back to First Officer
- Pay cuts, position sacrifice
- Recognition score becomes portable—capabilities travel, not airline seniority

---

## The Solution: PilotRecognition Platform

### Key Components
- **Recognition framework** (not a job board)
- **Three-part platform**: Programs, Pilot Recognition, Pathways
- **Pathway Cards** (not job listings) - show requirements and what you're missing
- **Pulling System** - pilots submit interest, airlines pull from database with live real-time profiles
- **Live Real-Time Profiles** (not static CVs) - profiles update as pilots log hours
- **Recognition Score as currency** - your score is what you spend on pathway access
- **EBT Video Scoring** - proprietary IP with behavioral scores (constructivism, cognitive thinking)
- **Two-tier model** - paid and free for advocacy

## Pricing Model Discussion (April 29, 2026)

### Initial Pricing Model Proposal

**User's initial proposal:**
- Access is free
- Pathways is free
- Mentorship is free
- Foundation program is free
- EBT video scoring: paid (due to storage costs)
- Pilots pay premium for shortlisting in pathway pooling
- Direct matching features
- Enterprise: airlines post pathways for free, pay $20 recognition fee per pilot hired

**Kevin O'Leary's Assessment:**
"Okay, now I understand. Here's what you're building:

**FREE for Pilots:**
- Platform access
- Pathways viewing
- Mentorship
- Foundation program

**PAID for Pilots:**
- EBT video scoring (storage costs)
- Premium shortlisting in pathway pooling
- Direct matching features

**PAID for Airlines/Operators:**
- Post pathways: FREE
- Success fee: $20 per pilot hired through pathway

**Kevin's Analysis:**

**1. The Good:**
- Freemium model drives user acquisition
- Success fee aligns incentives (you get paid when they get hired)
- Low barrier to entry for pilots (free to start)
- Free pathway posting encourages airline participation

**2. The Problems:**

**EBT video scoring as the main paid feature for pilots:**
- This is a niche feature. Not every pilot needs video scoring.
- You're limiting your revenue to a small subset of users.
- Storage costs shouldn't drive your pricing model. That's an operational detail, not a value proposition.

**Premium shortlisting:**
- What does this mean? If I pay, I get shown first?
- This undermines the recognition score as the primary filter.
- Recognition score should be the currency. Money shouldn't buy visibility.

**$20 success fee:**
- Too low. Airlines spend thousands on recruitment.
- $20 is noise to them.
- You're undervaluing your data.

**Kevin's Recommendation:**

**Pilot Pricing:**
- Keep access, pathways, mentorship, foundation program FREE
- Charge for the Foundational Program (not free) - this is your premium product
- EBT video scoring: bundle it with the program, don't charge separately

**Airline Pricing:**
- Post pathways: FREE (good)
- Pull data from database: $500/month subscription or $50 per pilot profile pulled
- Success fee: $500 per pilot hired through pathway (not $20)

**The Model Should Be:**

**Pilots:**
- Free tier: Access to platform, view pathways, basic profile
- Paid tier ($299/year or $49/month): Full Foundational Program, EBT video scoring, premium pathway access, priority matching

**Airlines:**
- Free tier: Post pathways, view pilot profiles
- Paid tier ($1,000/month): Pull API access, unlimited profile pulls, advanced filtering, dedicated account manager
- Success fee: $500 per pilot hired through pathway

**Kevin's Final Verdict:**

Your current model is too generous. You're giving away too much value for free. The Foundational Program should be your flagship paid product. EBT video scoring is a feature, not a product. Bundle it.

Charge airlines what your data is worth. $20 is an insult to your platform.

**Rethink the pricing. Then come back.**"
